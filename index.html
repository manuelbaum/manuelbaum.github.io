Manuel Baum

Robotics Researcher

Research

I am currently finishing my PhD at TU Berlin where my research is on robotic manipulation and mechanical problem solving in birds. In my research I program robots to perform mechanical tasks like opening drawers and grasping objects out of them. I am not only interested in how to let robots learn such behaviors, but also how to let them do so robustly -- e.g. when humans interfere with the task.

In my robotics research I have used lots of different techniques from machine learning, computer vision, and control theory. It's always interesting how methods from these fields can be adapted and integrated into robotic behavior. For example, methods from computer vision can benefit from the fact that robots can move their camera and remove occlusions by pushing obstacles out of the way.

Because robotic behavior is often not as robust as that of animals, I believe it is useful to understand how animals can solve similar mechanical problems. Thus, in collaboration with behavioral biologists I have also conducted research that spans the fields of robotics and biology. In this work we have investigated how Goffin's cockatoos can solve complex mechanical puzzles, so called lockboxes.
